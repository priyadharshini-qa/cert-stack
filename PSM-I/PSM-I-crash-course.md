## Scrum Definition

> Scrum is a **lightweight framework** that helps people, teams, and organizations generate value through adaptive solutions for complex problems.

**Scrum in a nutshell:**
1. A Product Owner orders work for a complex problem into a **Product Backlog**.
2. The Scrum Team turns a selection of that work into an **Increment** of value during a **Sprint**.
3. The Scrum Team and stakeholders **inspect** the results and **adapt** for the next Sprint.
4. **Repeat.**

**Key facts:**
- Scrum is **purposefully incomplete** — it only defines what's required to implement Scrum theory.
- Scrum **is not a methodology**; it's a framework within which you can employ various processes/techniques.
- Scrum **wraps around** existing practices or renders them unnecessary.
- "**Scrum is immutable**" — implementing only parts of it is possible, but the result is *not Scrum*.

## Scrum Theory

Scrum is founded on **two pillars of thought**:
- **Empiricism** — knowledge comes from experience; decisions are based on what is observed.
- **Lean thinking** — reduces waste, focuses on essentials.

### The Three Pillars of Empirical Process Control

| Pillar | Description |
|---|---|
| **Transparency** | The process and work must be visible to those doing the work and those receiving it. |
| **Inspection** | Artifacts and progress must be inspected frequently and diligently to detect undesirable variances. |
| **Adaptation** | If something is outside acceptable limits, it must be adjusted ASAP. |

**Logical chain:** Transparency *enables* Inspection → Inspection *enables* Adaptation → Adaptation requires empowered, self-managing people.

> "Inspection without transparency is misleading and wasteful."
> "Inspection without adaptation is considered pointless."

## Scrum Values

**Commitment, Focus, Openness, Respect, Courage**

| Value | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Commitment** | The team commits to its goals and to supporting each other. |
| **Focus** | Primary focus is on the work of the Sprint. |
| **Openness** | Team and stakeholders are open about the work and challenges. |
| **Respect** | Team members respect each other as capable, independent people. |
| **Courage** | Team has courage to do the right thing and tackle tough problems. |

> When these values are embodied, the pillars of Transparency, Inspection, and Adaptation "come to life, building trust."

## Scrum Team

**Composition:** 1 Scrum Master + 1 Product Owner + Developers
**Size:** Typically **10 or fewer** people (no fixed minimum/maximum is officially mandated in the 2020 Guide — but the Guide notes smaller teams communicate better).

**Core characteristics:**
- **Self-managing** — they decide internally *who* does *what*, *when*, and *how*.
- **Cross-functional** — members collectively have all skills needed to create value.
- **No sub-teams or hierarchies.**
- One cohesive unit focused on **one objective at a time: the Product Goal.**
- Accountable for **all** product-related activities: stakeholder collaboration, maintenance, operations, experimentation, research, etc.
- If too large → should split into multiple Scrum Teams sharing the same Product Goal, Product Backlog, and Product Owner.

### The Three Accountabilities

#### 1. Developers
Accountable for:
- Creating the **Sprint Backlog** (the plan).
- Instilling quality by adhering to the **Definition of Done**.
- Adapting their plan daily toward the **Sprint Goal**.
- Holding each other accountable as professionals.

#### 2. Product Owner
Accountable for **maximizing the value** of the product. Manages the Product Backlog by:
- Developing & communicating the **Product Goal**.
- Creating & communicating Product Backlog items.
- **Ordering** Product Backlog items.
- Ensuring the Product Backlog is transparent and understood.

> The Product Owner is **one person, not a committee**. They may delegate the work, but remain accountable. To change the backlog, stakeholders must convince the Product Owner.

#### 3. Scrum Master
Accountable for establishing Scrum as defined in the Guide, and for the **Scrum Team's effectiveness**.

Serves the **Scrum Team** by:
- Coaching self-management & cross-functionality.
- Helping focus on high-value Increments meeting the Definition of Done.
- **Causing the removal of impediments.**
- Ensuring all events happen, are positive, productive, and timeboxed.

Serves the **Product Owner** by:
- Helping find techniques for Product Goal definition & backlog management.
- Facilitating stakeholder collaboration.

Serves the **Organization** by:
- Leading/training/coaching Scrum adoption.
- Removing barriers between stakeholders and Scrum Teams.

> Scrum Masters are **true leaders who serve.**

## Scrum Events

> "All events are formal opportunities to inspect and adapt." Held at the same time/place to reduce complexity.

| Event | Timebox (1-month Sprint) | Purpose | Who |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Sprint** | ≤ 1 calendar month | Container for all other events; delivers value | Whole Scrum Team |
| **Sprint Planning** | ≤ 8 hours | Lays out the work for the Sprint; **initiates** the Sprint | Whole Scrum Team (+ invited advisors) |
| **Daily Scrum** | ≤ 15 minutes | Inspect progress toward Sprint Goal; re-plan next 24h | **Developers** (PO/SM only participate as Developers if doing Sprint Backlog work) |
| **Sprint Review** | ≤ 4 hours | Inspect the Increment outcome; adapt the Product Backlog | Scrum Team + stakeholders |
| **Sprint Retrospective** | ≤ 3 hours | Plan ways to increase quality & effectiveness; **closes** the Sprint | Whole Scrum Team |

*(All timeboxes scale down proportionally for shorter Sprints.)*

### The Sprint — Rules During It
- **No changes** that would endanger the Sprint Goal.
- **Quality does not decrease.**
- The Product Backlog is **refined as needed**.
- **Scope may be clarified/renegotiated** between PO and Developers as more is learned.
- **Only the Product Owner** can cancel a Sprint (if the Sprint Goal becomes obsolete).

### Sprint Planning — 3 Topics
1. **Why is this Sprint valuable?** → PO proposes value; team crafts the **Sprint Goal**.
2. **What can be Done this Sprint?** → Developers select Product Backlog items.
3. **How will the chosen work get done?** → Developers plan the work (often broken into items ≤1 day).

> Output of Sprint Planning = **Sprint Goal + selected PBIs + delivery plan** = **Sprint Backlog**.

### Daily Scrum
- For the **Developers** only.
- Same time/place every working day.
- Structure is up to the Developers — focus is progress toward the Sprint Goal.
- Not the *only* time plans can be adapted.

### Sprint Review
- A **working session**, not just a presentation.
- Reviews what was accomplished + what's changed in the environment.
- Product Backlog **may be adjusted**.
- Second-to-last event of the Sprint.

### Sprint Retrospective
- Looks at: individuals, interactions, processes, tools, Definition of Done.
- Identifies improvements — most impactful ones addressed ASAP (can go into next Sprint Backlog).
- **Concludes/closes** the Sprint.

## Scrum Artifacts

| Artifact | Represents | Commitment |
|---|---|---|
| **Product Backlog** | Emergent, ordered list of what's needed to improve the product | **Product Goal** |
| **Sprint Backlog** | Plan by and for the Developers (why, what, how) | **Sprint Goal** |
| **Increment** | A concrete, usable stepping stone toward the Product Goal | **Definition of Done** |

### Product Backlog
- **Single source of work** for the Scrum Team.
- **Refinement** = breaking down/defining items into smaller, more precise items (ongoing activity).
- Items "ready" once they can be Done within one Sprint.
- **Sizing/estimation is done by the Developers** (PO may influence via trade-off discussions).

### Sprint Backlog
- Composed of: **Sprint Goal (why)** + **selected PBIs (what)** + **delivery plan (how)**.
- **Highly visible, real-time** picture of Developers' planned work.
- Updated throughout the Sprint as more is learned.

### Increment
- Must be **usable** to provide value.
- Each Increment is **additive** to all prior Increments.
- **Multiple Increments** can be created within a single Sprint.
- An Increment **can be released to stakeholders before the Sprint ends** — Sprint Review is **never a gate** to releasing value.
- Work isn't part of an Increment **unless it meets the Definition of Done**.

## Commitments

| Commitment | Belongs To | Key Rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Product Goal** | Product Backlog | Long-term objective; team pursues **one Product Goal at a time** — must fulfill or abandon before taking the next. |
| **Sprint Goal** | Sprint Backlog | Single Sprint objective; created during Sprint Planning; provides flexibility on *how* it's achieved. |
| **Definition of Done** | Increment | Formal description of quality standards. If org-wide standard exists, **all Scrum Teams must follow it as a minimum**. If not, the Scrum Team creates one. Multiple teams on one product **must share the same DoD**. |

> A PBI not meeting the DoD **cannot be released or even presented at the Sprint Review** — it returns to the Product Backlog.

## Common Exam Traps

- **"Scrum Master manages the team"** → ❌ False. The team is **self-managing**; the SM *serves* and *coaches*.
- **"The Product Owner writes all the user stories"** → ❌ False. PO is accountable for the backlog but **may delegate** the writing.
- **"Daily Scrum is a status meeting for the boss"** → ❌ False. It's *by and for* the Developers, focused on the Sprint Goal.
- **"You need a burndown chart"** → ❌ False. The Guide says forecasting tools (burn-down/up, cumulative flow) are *useful but not mandatory* — they don't replace empiricism.
- **"Sprint Review = demo only"** → ❌ False. It's a **working/collaborative session**, not just a presentation.
- **"Only Developers can cancel a Sprint"** → ❌ False. **Only the Product Owner** can cancel a Sprint.
- **"Scrum Master is on the team only to remove blockers"** → ❌ Incomplete. They also coach, facilitate, and serve the *organization*, not just the team.
- **"You can skip the Retrospective if there's nothing wrong"** → ❌ False. It's mandatory regardless — it's how the Sprint is formally closed.
- **"Increments can only be released at Sprint Review"** → ❌ False. They may be released **anytime** during the Sprint.
- **"Scrum has project managers"** → ❌ Scrum has **no project manager role** — accountabilities are split across PO, SM, and Developers.

## Quick-Fire Q&A

**Q: Who can change the Sprint Backlog during the Sprint?**
A: The Developers — it's their plan, updated as they learn more.

**Q: Who decides the Definition of Done if the org has no standard?**
A: The Scrum Team creates one appropriate for the product.

**Q: What happens to a PBI that doesn't meet the DoD?**
A: It returns to the Product Backlog — it cannot be part of the Increment.

**Q: Can the Scrum Team change the Sprint Goal mid-Sprint?**
A: No — scope can be renegotiated, but **not at the expense of the Sprint Goal** itself.

**Q: What are Scrum's two foundational ideas?**
A: Empiricism and Lean thinking.

**Q: What's the relationship between the 3 artifacts and 3 commitments?**
A: Product Backlog → Product Goal | Sprint Backlog → Sprint Goal | Increment → Definition of Done.
